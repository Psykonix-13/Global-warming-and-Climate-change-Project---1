Global Warming & Climate Change

Understanding the Threat and How We Can Help

Introduction

What is Global Warming?

Global warming refers to the long-term rise in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Why Should We Care?

The impacts of global warming are severe, including rising sea levels, extreme weather, and loss of biodiversity. Learn more on Wikipedia.

What Can We Do?

We can reduce our carbon footprint, use renewable energy, and support policies aimed at reducing emissions. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Causes of Global Warming

Greenhouse Gases

Greenhouse gases, such as CO2 and methane, trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Deforestation

Deforestation reduces the number of trees that absorb CO2, contributing to higher carbon levels in the atmosphere. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Fossil Fuels

Burning fossil fuels for energy releases large amounts of CO2 and other greenhouse gases. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Effects of Global Warming

Rising Sea Levels

Melting ice caps and glaciers contribute to rising sea levels, threatening coastal communities. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Extreme Weather

Global warming is linked to more frequent and severe weather events, such as hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Impact on Wildlife

Many species are threatened by habitat loss and changing ecosystems due to global warming. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Solutions to Combat Global Warming

Renewable Energy

Switching to renewable energy sources like solar and wind can significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Energy Efficiency

Improving energy efficiency in buildings, transportation, and industries can help lower carbon emissions. Learn more on Wikipedia.

Reforestation

Planting trees and restoring forests can help absorb CO2 and mitigate climate change. Learn more on Wikipedia.
